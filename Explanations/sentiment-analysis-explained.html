What Is BERT? A Transformer Primer
The architecture that revolutionized NLP explained from scratch
▼

The Problem BERT Solves

Before 2018, NLP models treated words mostly in isolation. TF-IDF literally counts word frequencies, "not good" becomes two separate features: "not" (negative-ish) and "good" (positive), confusing models. RNNs processed words sequentially but struggled with long documents.

BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) changed everything by understanding words in context, processing entire sentences at once.

The Transformer Architecture

BERT is built on the Transformer (2017, "Attention Is All You Need"). The key innovation: self-attention- every word can directly attend to every other word in the sentence, regardless of distance.

🔑 Self-Attention in One Sentence: For each word, the model asks "which OTHER words in this sentence are relevant to understanding ME?" and computes weighted connections to all of them simultaneously.

For example, in "The movie was not good, but the acting saved it":

WordAttends Strongly ToWhy
"not""good"Negation modifies meaning
"but""not", "saved"Signals contrast/reversal
"it""movie"Coreference resolution
"saved""acting", "movie"What saved what

How Attention Actually Works : Q, K, V

Each word gets three vectors: a Query ("what am I looking for?"), a Key ("what do I contain?"), and a Value ("what do I output?"). The attention score between two words is computed by multiplying the Query of one with the Key of the other. High score = strong attention.

Multi-head attention runs this process 8–12 times in parallel (different "heads"), each learning different patterns: one head might focus on grammar, another on sentiment, another on negation.

How BERT Was Pre-Trained

BERT was pre-trained on 3.3 billion words (English Wikipedia + BookCorpus) using two tasks:

1. Masked Language Model

Randomly hide 15% of words → predict them from context. "The cat [MASK] on the mat" → "sat". Forces understanding from BOTH directions (bidirectional).

2. Next Sentence Prediction

Given two sentences, predict if B follows A. Teaches BERT to understand relationships between sentences.

📌 TF-IDF vs BERT The Core Difference: TF-IDF sees text as a bag of words - counts and frequencies, no word order, no context. BERT sees text as a structured sequence - it understands "not good" is negative even though "good" alone is positive. This contextual understanding is why BERT dominates.

Ensemble - Combining the Best of Both

Soft-voting (averaging LogReg + BERT probabilities) reached 92.4%better than either alone (+0.7% over BERT). They make different mistakes: BERT won 370 cases LogReg missed; LogReg won 223 cases BERT missed.

LIME Explainability

LIME reveals which words drove BERT's predictions. For one negative review, BERT's top signals were: "not" (−0.34), "difficult" (−0.19), "uninspiring" (−0.02) — confirming BERT reads context correctly.

Error Analysis

Mixed-sentiment reviews are hardest — they make up 21.7% of data but 32.8% of errors (over-represented by 1.5×). Reviews praising some aspects while criticizing others confuse even BERT.

Domain Generalization

Both TF-IDF and BERT scored 100% on 10 hand-crafted Amazon-style product reviews — sentiment understanding transfers across domains.

🎯 Takeaway #1 — Transfer Learning Dominates NLP. Zero-shot BERT (91.7%) beats every TF-IDF model (89.5%) despite never seeing a single IMDB example. Pre-trained language understanding beats word counting.
🎯 Takeaway #2 — Fine-Tuning Is Best of Both Worlds. Pre-trained BERT + task-specific training = 93.3%. Combining deep language understanding with domain knowledge.
🎯 Takeaway #3 — Speed vs Accuracy Is Real. TF-IDF is 1,573× faster than BERT on CPU. The right choice depends on your production constraints.
🎯 Takeaway #4 — Ensembles Beat Individuals. Averaging TF-IDF + BERT (92.4%) beats either alone — they fail on different review types.
🎯 Takeaway #5 — Same Story as Computer Vision. Just as MobileNetV2 (ImageNet) crushed training from scratch for pet breeds (77.8% vs 13.7%), BERT crushed TF-IDF for sentiment. Transfer learning is THE dominant paradigm across all of ML.
